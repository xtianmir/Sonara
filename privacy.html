Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 8, 2026 · ArtSolver Ltd.

♫ In plain words
  • Your music stays yours. Sonara never uploads or scans your files, library, playlists or listening history; all of that lives only on your computer.
  • No account is needed to use Sonara, not even for Premium.
  • The app sends one small anonymous usage ping per day (app version, Windows version, language and similar). You can switch it off in Settings at any time.
  • If you buy Premium, payment is handled by Polar; we never see your card details.
  • No ads, no trackers, no selling of data. Ever.

1.Who is responsible

The data controller for the processing described here is ArtSolver Ltd. (АртСолвър ЕООД), UIC 204535372, Bulgaria, the company of Sonara's creator Tihomir Nyagolov. Contact: .

2.The principle: local first

Sonara is a desktop player for your own music files. Your library, playlists, play history, ratings, lyrics and settings are stored only on your computer and are never transmitted to us or anyone else.

3.What the app does send, and when

3.1Anonymous usage statistics (can be turned off)

Once per day the app sends one small ping so we can see how many installs are active and which versions and languages matter most. It contains: app version, release channel, processor architecture, Windows version, install type (installer or portable), system language, the app language you chose, and an anonymous identifier.

  • The identifier is derived by one-way hashing from a machine ID. It cannot be reversed to identify you or your computer, and it is deliberately built so it cannot be linked to your license or purchase.
  • The ping contains no names, no file names, no music data, no listening history and no location, and your IP address is not stored with the statistics.
  • The data is stored on servers in the EU (Supabase, EU region).
  • Turning it off: Preferences, "Share anonymous usage statistics". Switching it off stops the pings immediately.
  • Legal basis: our legitimate interest in understanding basic usage of the app (GDPR art. 6(1)(f)), with an opt-out available at any time.

3.2Premium purchase (Polar)

Checkout is operated by Polar as merchant of record. Polar collects the data needed for the sale (email, country for tax purposes, payment details) under its own privacy policy. We never receive your payment details; we can see order information (your email and license key) needed for support and abuse prevention.

3.3License activation

When you activate a Premium key, the app sends to Polar: the key, your computer's name (so that you can recognize which of your 3 slots it occupies) and a one-way hashed machine fingerprint (used to enforce the 3-computer limit). Deactivating a computer frees the slot and removes that activation. The fingerprint is a hash: it does not identify you personally and cannot be linked to the anonymous usage statistics.

3.4Free trial

The 7-day trial is entirely local. Nothing about it is sent anywhere.

3.5Update checks

The app contacts GitHub, where releases are hosted, to check for and download updates. As with any internet request, GitHub sees your IP address and standard request metadata (GitHub's privacy policy applies). The app sends no identifiers with these requests.

3.6Discord "Now playing"

If the Discord desktop app is running on your computer, Sonara can pass the current track's title and artist to it, so your Discord profile shows what you are listening to. You can turn this off in Sonara at any time: Preferences, System, "Show activity on Discord". It also goes through your own Discord client, so your Discord privacy settings (Activity Privacy) apply as well. If Discord is not running, nothing is shared.

4.The website

  • The site is hosted on GitHub Pages; GitHub processes standard server logs (such as IP addresses) as the host.
  • We count page views with GoatCounter, a privacy-friendly, cookieless counter that stores no personal identifiers and shows us only aggregate numbers.
  • The site sets no advertising or tracking cookies.

5.What we never do

We do not collect your files or listening history, we do not profile you, we do not show ads, and we do not sell or share data with anyone beyond the processors named above (Polar, Supabase, GitHub).

6.How long data is kept

  • Anonymous usage pings: kept as statistics; they contain nothing that identifies you.
  • Order and invoice data at Polar: kept as long as tax and accounting law requires.
  • Activation records: until you deactivate the computer or the key.

7.Your rights

Under the GDPR you can request access, correction, deletion, restriction or portability of your data, and you can object to processing. Write to . One honest note: the usage statistics are anonymous, so we cannot tell which pings are "yours"; there is nothing we could look up or delete for a specific person. You can also lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority: in Bulgaria that is the Commission for Personal Data Protection (cpdp.bg), or the authority of your own country.

8.Changes to this policy

If a future feature starts sending or processing new data (for example optional online accounts), we will update this policy first and change the effective date above. Significant changes will be pointed out on the website or in the app.
